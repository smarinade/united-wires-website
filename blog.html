Minecraft Server Issues

Winter LAN Wrap-Up: The Great TNT Incident of '26

What started as a peaceful Saturday afternoon in our Minecraft server turned into absolute chaos at 2:47 AM. Kjell decided it was the perfect time to "test redstone mechanics" by building a massive TNT cannon aimed at... our main base.

Thirty seconds into his test fire, the server started lagging. By the time we realized what was happening, half our base was a crater. We lost 6 hours of building progress, our entire storage system, and Jeroen's prized enchanting setup.

After a heated debate about grief protection and an emergency pizza delivery, we implemented new rules: No TNT near the main base. No "redstone experiments" after midnight. Kjell is now permanently stationed on resource gathering duty.

Lessons learned: Always have backups enabled. Never trust Kjell with explosives. 3 AM is not the time for "testing mechanics."

Throwback Unreal Tournament

Throwback Thursday: The First United Wires LAN

Hard to believe it's been this long, but we're throwing it back to where it all started. Three friends, three computers, one basement, and a copy of Unreal Tournament 2004.

That first session was pure chaos. None of us had played UT2004 competitively before, and it showed. Sander discovered the shock rifle combo and proceeded to dominate for six straight hours. Jeroen kept falling off Facing Worlds. Kjell found the redeemer and... well, friendly fire was definitely on.

First Session Highlights:

  • Total playtime: 14 hours straight
  • Pizza boxes consumed: 4
  • Energy drinks: We lost count
  • Sander's instagib killstreak: 47 (still unbeaten)
  • Times Kjell killed himself with the redeemer: 23

That night, United Wires was born. We've evolved from UT2004 to strategy games, from three games to ten, but the core remains the same: three friends, epic gaming, and way too much caffeine.

Here's to 47 more LAN parties. GG WP, boys.

20 NOV 2025
Co-op Back 4 Blood

Back 4 Blood Nightmare: When Co-op Goes Catastrophic

"Let's try Back 4 Blood," Jeroen said. "It'll be a nice break from competitive games," he said. "Co-op will be relaxing," he promised.

Jeroen was spectacularly wrong.

Our first run went smoothly. The three of us blasted through Act 1 on Recruit difficulty, built decent card decks, extracted with minimal casualties. Confidence was high. Too high.

Run two: Nightmare difficulty. In retrospect, jumping from Recruit to Nightmare for our second-ever campaign might have been... ambitious. Here's how it went:

  • Minute 3: Kjell triggers a car alarm, summoning a horde
  • Minute 7: Sander shoots an explosive barrel, downs the entire team
  • Minute 12: We realize nobody brought healing cards
  • Minute 15: A Breaker spawns. We panic-fire everything.
  • Minute 16: Total party wipe. Game over.

We stared at the defeat screen in silence for a solid minute. Then someone ordered pizza and we tried again. And failed again. And again. And again.

Six hours and fifteen attempts later, we finally beat the first chapter. The gaming den erupted in cheers. Neighbors complained. Worth it.

Back 4 Blood is now a permanent fixture in our rotation. We've learned to embrace the chaos.
